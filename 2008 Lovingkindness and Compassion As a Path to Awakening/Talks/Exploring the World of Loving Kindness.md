##### Guild vs remorse
<span class="counts">**<a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="0130 Exploring the World of Loving Kindness > ^12-6" data-href="0130 Exploring the World of Loving Kindness#^12-6" class="internal-link">12-6</a>**: _<a data-href="The Self" class="internal-link">The Self</a> (5)_</span>

<span class="paragraph">A distinction between guilt and remorse. I don't know if these are dictionary definitions, but guilt, I would say, is when we remember something, we don't feel good about it, and <a data-href="the self" class="internal-link">the self</a> defines itself around it. It's making a conclusion about what we did - making a conclusion about <a data-href="the self" class="internal-link">the self</a>, rather. [laughter] ==Guilt is making a conclusion about <a data-href="the self" class="internal-link">the self</a>. Remorse, I would say, is making a conclusion about the action.== So guilt has a way of making definitions and binding oneself in a definition of <a data-href="the self" class="internal-link">the self</a>: "I'm bad. I'm useless. I'm a failure. I'm not a good person." Remorse is saying, "In the future, I would rather not act that way. I want to choose something different." It's saying something about the action. It's forward-looking, creative, open, whereas guilt is backward-looking and just stays around <a data-href="the self" class="internal-link">the self</a>.</span>
