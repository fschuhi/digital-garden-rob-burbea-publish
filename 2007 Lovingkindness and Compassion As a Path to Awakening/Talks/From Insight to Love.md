##### Metta is fabricated, but it's also the natural state
<span class="counts">**<a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="0127 From Insight to Love > ^1-2" data-href="0127 From Insight to Love#^1-2" class="internal-link">1-2</a>**: _<a data-href="Metta" class="internal-link">Metta</a> (6) 路 <a data-href="Love" class="internal-link">Love</a> (4) 路 <a data-href="Fabrication" class="internal-link">Fabrication</a> (3) 路 <a data-href="Compassion" class="internal-link">Compassion</a> (2) 路 <a data-href="Mind" class="internal-link">Mind</a> 路 <a data-href="Consciousness" class="internal-link">Consciousness</a> 路 <a data-href="Clinging" class="internal-link">Clinging</a> (2) 路 <a data-href="Ignorance" class="internal-link">Ignorance</a> (2) 路 <a data-href="Nature" class="internal-link">Nature</a> (3) 路 <a data-href="The Self" class="internal-link">The Self</a>_</span>
<span class="paragraph">It can seem at first that what we're doing in this practice is we're putting in lots of huff and puff, and building up some <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Metta" data-href="Metta" class="internal-link">mett</a>, creating some <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Metta" data-href="Metta" class="internal-link">mett</a>, creating [[love]]. ##### Insight is that which releases clinging
<span class="counts">**<a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="0127 From Insight to Love > ^1-3" data-href="0127 From Insight to Love#^1-3" class="internal-link">1-3</a>**: _<a data-href="Insight" class="internal-link">Insight</a> (6) 路 <a data-href="Meditation" class="internal-link">Meditation</a> 路 <a data-href="Clinging" class="internal-link">Clinging</a> (6) 路 <a data-href="Dukkha" class="internal-link">Dukkha</a> (2) 路 <a data-href="Buddha" class="internal-link">Buddha</a> 路 <a data-href="Ways of looking" class="internal-link">Ways of looking</a> (4) 路 <a data-href="Love" class="internal-link">Love</a> (2)_</span>
<span class="paragraph">When we talk about [[insight]], oftentimes we use this word, and we say '<a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Meditation" data-href="Meditation" class="internal-link">insight meditation</a>,' and sometimes it's sort of, "I wonder what's actually meant there?" <a data-href="Insight" class="internal-link">Insight</a>, to me, is that which releases [[clinging]]. Okay? So there's [[clinging]], there's constricting ourselves around something inner or outer, pushing it away, pulling it towards us, holding on to it, struggling with it. And [[clinging]] brings with it <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Dukkha" data-href="Dukkha" class="internal-link">suffering</a>. <a data-href="Insight" class="internal-link">Insight</a> is that which, either through intellectual <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Insight" data-href="Insight" class="internal-link">understanding</a>, through reflection, just an intuitive sense, some very organic sense, at any level at all (fairly easy or superficial, right down to the depths of <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Insight" data-href="Insight" class="internal-link">understanding</a>), releases that [[clinging]] and therefore releases <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Dukkha" data-href="Dukkha" class="internal-link">suffering</a>. So that's what [[insight]] is. The <a data-href="Buddha" class="internal-link">Buddha</a>'s enormously interested in this, and he's interested in <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Ways of looking" data-href="Ways of looking" class="internal-link">ways of seeing</a>, <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Ways of looking" data-href="Ways of looking" class="internal-link">ways of seeing</a> ourselves, our lives, the moment, <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Ways of looking" data-href="Ways of looking" class="internal-link">ways of seeing</a> and relating, that bring this release. And with the release, with the release from [[clinging]], comes [[love]]. So [[ways of looking]] that bring a release of [[clinging]] and bring with that [[love]].</span>

##### Constrictions block our capacity to love
<span class="counts">**<a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="0127 From Insight to Love > ^1-4" data-href="0127 From Insight to Love#^1-4" class="internal-link">1-4</a>**: _<a data-href="Metta" class="internal-link">Metta</a> 路 <a data-href="Compassion" class="internal-link">Compassion</a> 路 <a data-href="Insight" class="internal-link">Insight</a> 路 <a data-href="Clinging" class="internal-link">Clinging</a> (2) 路 <a data-href="Aversion" class="internal-link">Aversion</a> (2) 路 <a data-href="Love" class="internal-link">Love</a> (6) 路 <a data-href="Fear" class="internal-link">Fear</a> (6) 路 <a data-href="Constriction" class="internal-link">Constriction</a> 路 <a data-href="Anger" class="internal-link">Anger</a>_</span>
<span class="paragraph">And so what I want to go into a little bit is perhaps a possibility of incorporating some of that into the <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Metta" data-href="Metta" class="internal-link">mett</a> practice and the practice of [[compassion]]. So we can see in our lives (and hopefully this should get more and more clear as our <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Insight" data-href="Insight" class="internal-link">understanding</a> deepens): when there is [[clinging]] or [[aversion]], there's less [[love]]. ##### Finding a dying man in the sewers of Calcutta
<span class="counts">**<a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="0127 From Insight to Love > ^2-3" data-href="0127 From Insight to Love#^2-3" class="internal-link">2-3</a>**: _<a data-href="Mahatma Gandhi" class="internal-link">Mahatma Gandhi</a>_</span>
<span class="paragraph">So he took that, he took <a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="Mahatma Gandhi" data-href="Mahatma Gandhi" class="internal-link">Gandhi</a> at his words, and he got a job down there cleaning the sewers in Calcutta. You can imagine. He decided to do that for a year. And one day, he was coming home at night in the dark from a day's working in the sewers, and he tripped over something in the road. He looked down, and he saw that it was a man lying in the road, abandoned and dying of leprosy in its final stages. He saw this man there and was so horrified that he ran home. Immediate kind of knee-jerk response, and he ran to his home. And when he arrived home, kind of out of breath, he got there and then he realized, "Oh, what have I done?" ##### Maharashtra

##### Don't push away fear, but incline the awareness a bit towards loveliness
<span class="counts">**<a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="0127 From Insight to Love > ^2-5" data-href="0127 From Insight to Love#^2-5" class="internal-link">2-5</a>**: _<a data-href="Fear" class="internal-link">Fear</a> (12) 路 <a data-href="Love" class="internal-link">Love</a> 路 <a data-href="Samadhi" class="internal-link">Samadhi</a> 路 <a data-href="Mind" class="internal-link">Mind</a> (4) 路 <a data-href="Awareness" class="internal-link">Awareness</a> 路 <a data-href="Pleasantness" class="internal-link">Pleasantness</a> 路 <a data-href="Aversion" class="internal-link">Aversion</a>_</span>

##### Fear involves unpleasant body sensations and a constriction of the mind
<span class="counts">**<a aria-label-position="top" aria-label="0127 From Insight to Love > ^3-1" data-href="0127 From Insight to Love#^3-1" class="internal-link">3-1</a>**: _<a data-href="Fear" class="internal-link">Fear</a> (5) 路 <a data-href="Embodiment" class="internal-link">Embodiment</a> 路 <a data-href="Constriction" class="internal-link">Constriction</a> (2) 路 <a data-href="Mind" class="internal-link">Mind</a> 路 <a data-href="Consciousness" class="internal-link">Consciousness</a> (5) 路 <a data-href="Spaciousness" class="internal-link">Spaciousness</a> 路 <a data-href="Aversion" class="internal-link">Aversion</a>_</span>
<span class="paragraph">So, often we run away from [[fear]], we don't even stop to wait and investigate a little bit: what actually is this that I'm running away from? What actually is [[fear]]?
